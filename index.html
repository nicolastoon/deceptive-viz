Design Decisions and Rationale:

  • Using hotter colors for greater magnitude extremes (2): This design choice in color is super important because it gets the reader to remember the colors that stand out to them. Since one of the boroughs is a color that stands out compared to the rest, they are more likely to remember that borough over the others.
  • Using a diverging color scale (1): This decision is persuasive because it makes the boroughs on the extremes a very different colors. However, I would say that this design decision is not completely persuasive since the median doesn't really stand for anything, but a diverging color scale implies that the median does hold some sort of "neutral" value.
  • Choosing total number of complaints as the metric (0.5): At first glance, this metric seems pretty appropriate for the question. More complaints = worse police officers. However, the metric does fail to account for the number of police officers in each borough. The number of complaints will naturally be higher in areas where there are more police officers.
